### Large Scale Electron-Lithography
In academia on the other hand, where we do not need mass-manufacturing but instead prefer high-precision and control for a low price, electron-lithography is used more commonly. 



If one were to challenge ASML with its large and costly machines which are absurdely expensive and incredibly difficult to build and manage, electron-lithography may be an easy solution as we approach 1 nm resolutions since its only problem is not resolution but speed and throughput. I don't see why we couldn't fix that by simply adding more electron beams that work in parallel and designing these machines not for manual control but to process wafers quickly and fully automated. They are fundamentally simpler and yet more precise.
