### Comment
Right after my B.Sc., having formulated a new approach to modelling charge carrier dynamics in nanostructures as probed by THz spectroscopy, we realized something I had ignored for my B.Sc. thesis, that is, the field strength of the THz pulse actually causes a non-linear response! This was quite unexpected for us at first since we still operated under the outdated assumption that we were working with a linear-response theory as common in the literature. But since I had made no approximations and stuck to a numerical approach in my B.Sc., the equations were parametric / tightly coupled to the electric field of the THz pulse. Several smaller discoveries were made as well, for example, that the Ehrenfest theorem did not hold true in our case. Considering this, we were also able to explain a previously predicted classical equilibration current as a quantum mechanical effect which counteracts the mobility at low frequencies. Cool stuff!
