My goal for this blog post is to document and summarize my attempt to learn electrical engineering to the point that I am able to creatively apply these skills to build whatever comes to my mind if given enough time and the needed recources. Among other things, I aim to design, build, and test a simple central processing unit with its own assembly language.



First, I want to work through the book _Practical Electronics for Engineers_ by Scherz and Monk[^1] which honestly seems awesome as it skips over most of the physics which I already understand deeply and dives right into electronics, giving an extremely wide overview within almost 1000 pages. I love it and I am excited about it.


I also plan to learn to use Logisim[^2] more to try and get a better grasp of digital processors.

We will learn KiCad[^3] of course so we can create actual designs of integrated circuits and quickly produce and not hustling with ugly breadboards.

Learning VERILOG[^4] I imagine would also be quite practical!

And I am really excited about potentially implementing analog circuits to simulate physical phenomana like spiking neurons or other chaotic systems.
